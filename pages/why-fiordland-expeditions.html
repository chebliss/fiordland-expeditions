History & Heritage

Historic Fiordland

Shortly after COVID-19 temporarily closed our operations in March 2020, we were approached by the Otago Early Settlers Museum to assist in recalling the historic ventures of early European explorers in Fiordland.

Our first visit, in August 2020, was to Dusky Sound, where we explored the journeys of early explorers and settlers. Captain Cook spent 32 days in Dusky Sound recovering from a long sea voyage. During his time here, he charted the area and encountered some of the long-lost Ngāti Mamoe living in scattered family groups.

In late January 2022, we ventured further south to Preservation and Chalky Sounds, exploring the sites of early industries including gold mining, timber milling, and seal and whale hunting. Each visit has offered a fascinating glimpse into Fiordland's rich, layered history.
